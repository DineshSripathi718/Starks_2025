POTATO FRY

Potato Fry—also known as Aloo Fry—is a beloved Indian side dish that’s quick to make and packed with flavor. It’s essentially sautéed or shallow-fried potatoes seasoned with spices, and it pairs beautifully with rice, roti, or even as a sandwich filling.

Ingridients

- Potatoes (cubed or sliced) - Oil (mustard, peanut, or vegetable) - Spices: turmeric, red chili powder, coriander powder, cumin powder, garam masala - Tempering: mustard seeds, cumin seeds, curry leaves, garlic, and asafoetida (hing) - Optional: dry mango powder (amchur) or lemon juice for tang

Steps

  • Prep the potatoes: Peel and cube them evenly. Soak in water for 20–30 minutes to remove excess starch.
  • Tempering: Heat oil in a pan and add mustard seeds, cumin seeds, garlic, curry leaves, and hing.
  • Saute: Drain potatoes and add them to the pan. Cook on medium heat until half done.
  • Spice it up: Add turmeric, chili powder, coriander, cumin, and salt. Stir well.
  • Finish: Cook until potatoes are tender and slightly crispy. Add garam masala and amchur or lemon juice at the end
